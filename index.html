What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in the temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric conditions on Earth. The current climate crisis is driven by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes that release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. This results in global warming, rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and disruptions to ecosystems and biodiversity.

Climate Change in Numbers

1.2°C

Global temperature increase since pre-industrial times. Scientists warn that the world needs to limit warming to 1.5°C to avoid catastrophic effects.

18%

Percentage of global greenhouse gas emissions caused by deforestation and land-use changes.

12 years

Time remaining to limit global warming to 1.5°C and avoid the worst impacts of climate change, according to the IPCC.

What Can You Do?

Climate change is a global challenge, but individual actions matter! Here are some simple steps you can take to reduce your carbon footprint:

  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Cut down on waste and be mindful of what you buy and throw away.
  • Switch to Renewable Energy: Use clean energy sources like solar or wind power for your home.
  • Drive Less: Walk, bike, or take public transportation to reduce your carbon emissions.
  • Support Green Businesses: Choose products and services that prioritize sustainability.
  • Raise Awareness: Share information about climate change with friends and family, and encourage action.
